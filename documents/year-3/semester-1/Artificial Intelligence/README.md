## Reflection

Artificial Intelligence introduced problems that often have several possible approaches rather than one fixed solution. Through the assignments and proof-of-concept work, I examined how an AI problem can be defined, how a method can be selected, and how evidence should be used to evaluate the result. The subject encouraged more independent technical investigation.

One of the main challenges was connecting broad AI concepts with the specific requirements of each task. It was important to avoid assuming that an intelligent method was automatically suitable or successful. I learned to consider the problem context, available data, evaluation evidence, limitations, and the difference between a demonstration and a reliable deployed solution.

The subject strengthened my interest in AI-supported applications while also making me more critical of their results. It prepared me for the CNN tutorial and other projects involving AI integration. Most importantly, it taught me that responsible AI work requires clear objectives, transparent evaluation, and honest communication about what the evidence can and cannot demonstrate.
