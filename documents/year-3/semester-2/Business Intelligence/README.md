## Reflection

Business Intelligence focuses on turning structured business records into information that can support understanding and decision-making. Through the laboratory files and Olist project, I worked with spreadsheet and CSV data, transformation workflows, prepared outputs, and Power BI evidence. The subject demonstrates the connection between data preparation and visual communication.

The main challenge has been keeping fields consistent across source data, transformations, and reporting outputs. A dashboard may look clear while still being misleading if the underlying data is incomplete or incorrectly joined. This has taught me to inspect source records, document transformation choices, validate outputs, and connect each visual to a meaningful business question.

The subject has improved both my technical and communication skills. It shows that business intelligence is not only about designing charts but also about understanding data quality, context, and audience needs. These lessons support my wider Data Engineering development because reliable dashboards depend on well-designed processes for preparing and validating data.
