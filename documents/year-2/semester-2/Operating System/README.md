## Reflection

Operating System introduced the software layer responsible for managing hardware resources and supporting application execution. The course and project evidence helped me connect concepts such as resource management and system behaviour with a practical technical context. It broadened my understanding of what happens beneath the applications that users interact with.

The main challenge was moving from theoretical definitions to a clear explanation of how the concepts operate in practice. Operating-system processes are often hidden from normal users, so diagrams, examples, and careful investigation were important for making the ideas understandable. This encouraged me to examine the relationship between applications, resources, and the underlying environment.

The subject complemented my earlier computer-architecture knowledge and later performance-related studies. Understanding operating-system responsibilities helps me reason about memory use, execution, files, and resource constraints. These ideas became especially relevant when comparing data-processing frameworks and considering why the same task may behave differently across environments.
