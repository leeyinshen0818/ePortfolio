## Reflection

Data Structure and Algorithm developed my understanding of how information can be organised and processed efficiently. Through assignments, laboratory work, a lab test, and the mini project, I applied structures and algorithms to practical programming problems. The subject showed that two solutions may produce the same output while differing greatly in clarity, behaviour, or efficiency.

The main challenge was deciding which structure was appropriate and understanding how each operation changed its state. I needed to trace insertion, removal, searching, or traversal carefully and test the sequence with different inputs. The mini project made these concepts more meaningful by applying a queue to a complete simulation rather than treating it as an isolated exercise.

This subject strengthened my algorithmic thinking and prepared me for larger software and data-processing tasks. I became more aware of performance, ordering, and the trade-offs involved in selecting a structure. These ideas remain relevant when designing applications, querying data, processing large datasets, and evaluating whether an implementation will scale effectively.
