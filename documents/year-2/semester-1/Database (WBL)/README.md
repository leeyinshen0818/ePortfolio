## Reflection

Database (WBL) introduced the principles of organising structured information through relational databases. The assignments, SQL laboratories, and project helped me understand tables, keys, relationships, constraints, and queries. I learned that database design affects not only how information is stored but also how accurately and efficiently it can be retrieved.

A major challenge was maintaining data integrity while connecting several related tables. Incorrect keys, inconsistent values, or unsuitable relationships could produce unreliable results. Writing and checking SQL queries taught me to examine both the structure of the database and the meaning of the returned data instead of focusing only on whether a statement executed successfully.

This subject became one of the foundations of my Data Engineering studies. It developed my confidence in SQL and relational modelling and prepared me for Database Programming, business intelligence, and application projects that depend on stored data. It also taught me that reliable analysis begins with well-organised, consistent, and clearly defined data.
