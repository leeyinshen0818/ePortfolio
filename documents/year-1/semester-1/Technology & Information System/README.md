## Reflection

Technology & Information System broadened my understanding of computing beyond programming. The assignments introduced industry practices, organisational information systems, emerging technologies, professional communication, and design thinking. Preparing posters, reports, presentations, and reflections helped me examine how technology is selected and used to support real organisational needs.

A key challenge was deciding which information was most relevant and presenting it in a form that was clear to other people. Group activities required coordination, division of responsibilities, and consistent communication. I learned that technical knowledge is more valuable when it can be explained accurately and connected to a practical problem or user need.

The subject helped me see the wider context of the computing profession and encouraged me to explore different areas of technology. It strengthened my research, presentation, teamwork, and reflective-writing skills. These abilities remain useful in later projects because data engineering work also requires understanding stakeholders, communicating findings, and designing solutions that provide meaningful value.
