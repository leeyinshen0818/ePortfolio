# Digital Logic Laboratory

## Overview

This folder contains digital-logic laboratory reports, circuit files, diagrams, and VHDL evidence.

## Objectives

- Design and test digital circuits.
- Compare schematic and hardware-description approaches.

## Tools and Technologies

Digital circuit simulation files (`.pbs`), VHDL, images, and PDF reports.

## Folder Contents / Evidence

- [Lab 1 report](LAB1.pdf)
- [Lab 2 report](LAB2.pdf)
- Circuit and result images in this folder.
- [VHDL source](LAB3_VHDL)

## What I Did

I built, tested, and documented the assigned digital circuits.

To complete this laboratory task, I focused on linking logic concepts with the available circuit and VHDL evidence. I reviewed the available files and outputs together so that the implementation, written explanation, and supporting evidence remained connected to the task objectives. Where the evidence did not clearly describe a personal contribution, I kept the description limited to the work that can be verified from this folder.

## Key Learning Outcomes

I learned how logic expressions become circuit structures and how simulation helps verify behaviour.

The work also strengthened my understanding of digital logic, circuit behaviour, and the relationship between logical expressions and hardware implementation. I became more aware that a technical result should be supported by a clear process, suitable evidence, and an explanation of why the selected approach was appropriate. This will help me approach related tasks more systematically in future coursework.

## Reflection

The labs improved my attention to signal connections and truth-table behaviour. Small wiring errors could change the complete result.

The most important challenge was interpreting circuit behaviour carefully and checking that each logical condition produced the expected result. Working through this required me to check the task in smaller stages and compare the result with the available requirements or evidence. This process improved my patience and made me more careful about verifying technical work before presenting it.

Overall, this laboratory task contributed to my development by combining practical work with documentation and reflection. It showed me which parts I can now complete with more confidence and which areas still require further practice. The experience will be useful when I work on larger projects that demand clearer organisation, stronger testing, and more independent technical decisions.

## Possible Improvements

Label final circuit versions clearly. A useful next step would be to add truth tables beside each implementation.

In a future version, I would also focus on clearer circuit labels, simulation evidence, and a short explanation of how each output was verified. These additions would make the work easier to understand, reproduce, and evaluate while providing stronger evidence of the development process.
