## Reflection

Digital Logic introduced me to the fundamental ideas that allow computer hardware to represent and process information. Through logic gates, Boolean operations, circuit behaviour, and VHDL-related evidence, I learned that complex digital systems are built from smaller logical components. This subject strengthened my ability to follow conditions carefully and connect theoretical expressions with practical circuit outcomes.

One of the main challenges was visualising how a change in one input could affect several later outputs. I had to work systematically, check truth conditions, and compare expected behaviour with the available laboratory evidence. This process improved my attention to detail and showed me the value of testing each part of a design before evaluating the complete circuit.

Overall, the subject gave me a better understanding of the hardware foundation beneath programming and software systems. Although my later studies focus more strongly on data and applications, this knowledge helps me appreciate how instructions and information are represented at a lower level. It also developed the logical reasoning skills that I continue to use in programming, database work, and technical problem solving.
