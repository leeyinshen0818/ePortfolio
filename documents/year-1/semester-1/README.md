## Semester Learning

I began learning how to solve problems through structured programming, understand basic digital circuits, and examine the role of information systems in organisations. Programming Technique I introduced C++ and taught me to work with variables, control structures, functions, arrays, and files. Digital Logic helped me understand how logical operations and circuits support computer hardware.

Technology & Information System broadened my awareness of the technology industry through research, presentations, industry-related activities, and design thinking. Across the semester, I completed individual and group work involving source code, reports, posters, presentations, and practical exercises. These different forms of coursework helped me develop both technical knowledge and communication skills.

## Reflection

This semester was my first major step into computer science. Learning programming for the first time was challenging because small syntax or logic mistakes could stop an entire program from working. By practising regularly and checking problems one step at a time, I became more patient and systematic when debugging.

I also learned that computer science is not limited to writing code. Digital systems, information technology, teamwork, research, and user needs are equally important. Completing the semester gave me greater confidence in learning unfamiliar technical topics and provided a strong foundation for later programming and data-engineering subjects.
