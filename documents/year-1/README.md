# 🎓 Year 1

Welcome to my first year in the Bachelor of Computer Science (Data Engineering) programme at Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM).

Year 1 focused on developing a foundation in programming, computer systems, software design, mathematical thinking, and information technology.

## 📚 Academic Overview

During this year, I developed introductory knowledge in:

- C++ programming and problem solving
- Digital logic and computer architecture
- Human-computer interaction
- System analysis and design
- Information systems and technology

## 📝 Reflection

Year 1 introduced me to both the theoretical and practical sides of computing. At the beginning of the programme, many concepts were completely new to me, especially programming, digital logic, computer architecture, and formal system documentation. Through regular exercises and assignments, I learned to divide problems into manageable steps, test possible solutions, and correct mistakes patiently. Programming Technique I and II provided a foundation in C++, while Digital Logic and Computer Organization and Architecture helped me understand how software instructions relate to hardware operations.

The design and information-system subjects expanded my understanding beyond technical implementation. Human Computer Interaction taught me to consider usability and user expectations, while System Analysis and Design showed the importance of requirements, modelling, and planning. Technology & Information System introduced industry perspectives and encouraged me to think about how technology supports organisations and solves practical problems.

Year 1 also developed my communication, teamwork, and time-management skills through reports, presentations, programming tasks, and group projects. I sometimes found it difficult to balance several unfamiliar subjects, but the experience taught me to plan earlier, review feedback, and ask for clarification when necessary. By the end of the year, I was more confident in writing programs, explaining technical ideas, and approaching new problems systematically. This year became the foundation for my later studies in databases, software development, analytics, and data engineering.
